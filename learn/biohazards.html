Biohazards

Environmental biohazards, such as malaria and other infectious diseases, can be deadly to women and children.

We know that substandard sanitary conditions and exposure to disease can result in a weakened immune system, and often lead to miscarriages among pregnant women. These issues are compounded by exposure to environmental factors: contaminated water sources, poor sanitation, exposure to malaria-carrying mosquitos, etc. All of these things contribute to the high rates of infant and child mortality.

Fortunately, these issues can also be solved with some simple solutions: providing clean drinking water for everyone, improving sanitation and hygiene practices across the board, better education about mosquito-borne diseases. We have the opportunity to make great strides in raising healthy families here if only we provide the resources necessary to do so.
