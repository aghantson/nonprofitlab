Wake Up to Environmental Hazards in Maternal Health

Share Us, Spread the Word

Climate change is a public health crisis – one of the most pressing issues facing the world today. It threatens not only our future but our present health, as well.
Women who live in poor communities of color are the most vulnerable to climate change's effects on maternal health and reproductive outcomes.

Share

The effects of air pollution on pregnant women and their babies are far-reaching.
From the moment of conception until the day a mother gives birth, the air she breathes has an impact on her and her child's health.

Share

Toxic chemicals are all around us.
With each breath you take, you're inhaling a cocktail of toxic chemicals. And with each bite you eat, you're ingesting them. With every surge of adrenaline, your body is absorbing them.
Whether it is from air pollution from traffic, or toxic runoff in our water supplies, fertilizers, pesticides, and other agricultural chemicals—to the chemicals we use in our homes and personal care products—toxic chemicals are accumulating in our environment every day.

Share

Environmental injustice can take many forms.
In addition to affecting lower-income communities, it also disproportionately affects indigenous populations. Communities suffering from environmental injustice often face issues related to their health, such as increased rates of lead poisoning or asthma.
